Frequently asked questions

What’s intriguing about the Hamilton Beach TrueAir air purifier?

The first thing I noticed when I examined this small number were the dimensions (so small) and the requirements for replacing the filters (not a single one). Promotional photos of the air purifier show that it stands comfortably on a small shelf and doesn’t look bigger than a small stereo system.

The next thing that made me scratch and frown on my head is the HEPA filter. As a consumer, take note of everything that is advised to you, contrary to the truth, so let us clarify this point. Yes, the filter is of HEPA quality, but this is not true HEPA. A true HEPA filter captures particles up to 0.3 microns, but the filter in this model captures particles up to 3 microns. This usually means that the filter is designed with a HEPA dust and construction, but that the filter does not work at exactly the same level as the actual filter.

I’m not saying Hamilton Beach is meant to deceive you or to allude to you. But many companies use the “HEPA business card” to offer the concept that their product contains a real HEPA filter. When you read the specifications to get a quick purchase, it is very easy to think “big, HEPA filtration” if this is not quite the situation.
