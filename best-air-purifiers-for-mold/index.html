What is the difference between a mold air purifier and a dehumidifier?

A dehumidifier removes moisture from the air. If you’ve ever lived in a cold, damp house, you’ll understand the feeling of cold air humidity that you can practically feel your hands wet when you let them pass through the air. A dehumidifier removes and retains moisture. This helps to kill and reduce the total amount of mold in the room, since mold appreciates moisture.

An air purifier does not treat humidity. On the contrary, it brings air to the filter, eliminates airborne spores and releases clean, clean air. A dehumidifier has a bucket that collects moisture (it can be alarming to see exactly how far away).

The two can go hand in hand, but do not do exactly the same job or send out exactly the same results.

What kind of mold do you have in your house?

There are more than 400,000 species of mold on Earth. Fortunately, only a thousand of them can live in a household. All these species are divided into three classes.

Allergens. All of these are only dangerous if you have allergies or respiratory problems, such as asthma. Young children are particularly vulnerable to airborne spores.

Pathogen. These are dangerous species that can cause infections. They don’t affect 100% healthy people, but when your immune system is affected, they can cause severe lung disease.

Toxic. This is really the most dangerous group that contains the famous black mold. This group can affect anyone. They suppress the immune system and can cause cancer. All these species create mycotoxins that are harmful to health when ingested or inhaled. It is possible to get side effects after touching them.

In our homes we are mainly confronted with five types of mold. They are:

  • Alernaria – occurs on the nose, mouth and upper respiratory tract and can cause allergic reactions.
  • Aspergillus – Normally observed in extremely humid environments. It settles in house dust and can produce mycotoxins, which cause many lung diseases.
  • Cladosporium – This is a quick and easy way to remove fabrics, wood and other porous surfaces. It can cause hay fever and asthma.
  • Penicillium — likes to grow on the bottom, carpet and fiberglass insulation. It can cause asthma and allergies. Can produce mycotoxins.
  • Stachybotrys – Toxic form or black form that leads to mycotoxin and causes serious problems, such as lung bleeding. It is not as common in households as in the other four types of households. It can be applied on paper or wood, but perhaps not on tiles or concrete.

What needs to be done to remove mold?

Mold particles are small enough to pass through the eyelashes of your sinuses and also small enough to be easily exhaled so that they can nestle in your lung tissue. Larger particles, such as dust, are often captured by the eyelashes until they penetrate the lungs.

Exposure to mold can be dangerous

Some mold, known as mycotoxins, are products of the mold metabolism and can be toxic. Allergic reactions to fungus are usually runny nose, sneezing, red eyes, rash and symptoms usually associated with colds or hay fever. Symptoms often appear immediately, but can sometimes be delayed by hours. People with asthma can also have allergic reactions to mold. Infants, children and the elderly can suffer a lot from mold inhalation compared to healthy adults.

The mold spores are microscopic

Most mold spores are between 3 and 100 microns in size, but some can be as small as 1 microns. If you look at air purifiers that remove mold, it is very important to invest in a treatment that removes at least 3 microns of particles of a smaller size. HEPA air purifiers are really an excellent method to remove mold spores from the indoor air. In fact, our product experts specifically recommend HEPA air purifiers with this particular problem, and it is one of the best air purification technologies available to combat fungus, at AirPurifiers.com.

Mold can be difficult to remove

Even if you can’t completely get rid of mold in your home, it can be significantly reduced. If your house is old, the risk of mold problems is greater. Newer, watertight houses that do not allow sufficient air circulation can also be problematic. Office buildings can also often be contaminated with mold.

The removal of mold can be a constant process

Small visible areas of the fungus should be cleaned with household purifiers and also with dried areas. On the other hand, mold is often not visible because it is found in hidden places. Mold may be on a surface, but when the surface is interrupted, the spores spread through the air and begin to cause problems for individuals. Normally the spores settle over time, but when disturbed, they are absorbed in the airstream. This is where continuous operation or air purifiers form mold or can be of great help to get a clean interior.

Prevention Of Mold And Mildew

Killing mold with your air purifier is a good prevention. To increase the effectiveness of mold and mildew prevention, you can count on the following activities.

Repair of leaks: Leaking pipes or roof systems can increase the humidity in your home and create an ideal environment for mold formation. Repair dry areas and clear wet areas immediately.

Cleaning after the flood: Don’t just leave your cellar under water, as it’s a huge source of moisture for mold growth. Post-flood cleaning is essential to keep your cellar dry.

Check the air outlet: The kitchen and bathroom are the most likely places for mold growth. Make sure that the air vent works as well as possible to remove moisture from the outside of the house.
